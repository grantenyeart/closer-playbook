How we turn a skeptical lead into a signed deal.

The Mindset

Doctor, not salesman.

You don't pitch — you diagnose, then prescribe. The buyer should feel understood before they ever feel sold.

1
Diagnose first
Ask before you tell. No prescription without a real diagnosis.
2
Sell the outcome
Nobody buys ads or software. They buy more jobs, time back, a sellable business.
3
Control the frame
You set the agenda. Calm authority closes — eagerness repels.
🎤 Talk track

Start here. If they remember one thing today: we diagnose before we prescribe. A salesman pitches; a doctor asks questions. Doctors get trusted — and paid.

The Call Frame

Every call runs the same 7 moves

1 Take Control — set the agenda in 60 sec
2 Dig for Pain — questions before any pitch
3 Get Their Number — budget before you quote
4 Sell the Dream — outcomes, not features
5 Ask for the Close — assume the yes
6 Isolate Objections — find the real one
7 Lock the Next Step — a date, not a "maybe"
🎤 Talk track

These seven never change, and the order matters. Skip discovery and you'll be pitching a stranger. Memorize this list — it's the skeleton of every call you'll ever run.

Phase 1 · Control

Own the first 60 seconds

  • Positions you as the authority, not the salesman
  • Pre-frames your questions so they don't feel interrogated
  • Earns you permission to push back later
  • "If it's not a fit, I'll tell you" = power to walk away
Say this
"Hey [name] — before we dive in, here's how I run these calls: I'll ask you a handful of questions so I actually understand your business, then I'll tell you straight whether we're a fit. Sound fair?"
🎤 Talk track

The first minute decides who's driving. Say the agenda line out loud, every time — it earns you the right to ask the hard questions that come next.

Phase 2 · Discovery

Make them say the number out loud

Current
$3,500/wk
Where are you now?
Goal
$5,000/wk
Where do you want to be?
The Gap
+$1,500/wk
This is what you sell to

You can't sell a destination they haven't named. Get current, goal, and the gap on the table before you pitch anything.

🎤 Talk track

Don't pitch until you have three numbers. The gap is the whole sale — everything you propose is just the bridge across it.

Discovery · The Questions

5 questions that uncover the pain

1
"Walk me through a typical week — how many jobs, what's the mix?"
Baseline
2
"What's working you wish was bigger? What's broken you wish was fixed?"
Pain + opportunity
3
"If you doubled in 12 months — more vans? a shop? selling it?"
The dream
4
"What's stopping that from happening today?"
The wall = your angle
5
"What have you tried before, and what did it cost you?"
Sunk cost + budget signal
🎤 Talk track

Ask, then shut up. Your job is to make them say the pain twice. Pain said once is a fact; pain said twice is a motive you can close on.

Phase 3 · Budget

Get their number before you quote yours

Ask, then go silent
"What have other companies quoted you for this? Or what would you put toward the right partner?"
Then shut up. First one to talk loses.
Anchor to their wallet — not your price sheet.
A client told us he'd been paying $1k/mo + ad spend. We anchored against that.
If a buyer hands you a budget, your offer should fill it. Don't sell $30 to a $5,000 buyer.
🎤 Talk track

Never quote before you know their number. If they had five grand for the last agency, sell up to five grand — not down to your cheapest option.

Phase 4 · The Pitch

Sell the dream, not the dashboard

S
Story
"A detailer just like you went from X to Y in six weeks. Here's what we did…"
M
Math
"5 extra jobs a week × your ticket = $X/mo. The math works whether they believe you or not."
V
Vision
"Phone's booked by 8am, you're home by 5, and winter's handled. That's what I sell."

Features tell. Outcomes close. Nobody buys "CRM tracking" — they buy their Sunday back.

🎤 Talk track

Stop listing features. Story → math → vision, in that order. Paint the day their life looks like in 90 days and let them step into it.

Phase 5 · The Ask

Ask for the deal — 3 times

Assumptive
"Only two things left — send your COI and we go live Friday. Best email?"
Hard assumptive
"I've got the agreement open on my screen — spell your LLC name for me."
Two-option
"Start Monday with data before your contract ends, or wait 8 weeks and start cold. Which one?"

"Think about it" is not on the menu. You haven't sold until you've asked them to buy.

🎤 Talk track

You must ask — out loud — at least three times in a call. Most reps never ask once. Assume the yes and make them say no if they mean it.

Phase 6 · Objections

Isolate before you answer

1
Isolate
"Other than [their objection], is there anything else stopping us today?"
2
Confirm
"So if I solve [that], we move forward today — yes?"
3
Now swing
Answer the ONE real objection — once, with precision.

The first objection is rarely the real one. Isolate it so you swing once — not five times.

🎤 Talk track

Don't answer the first objection — it's usually a smokescreen. Isolate it, get the commitment, then you only have one fight instead of five.

Objection Playbook

The 6 you'll hear most

"I need to think about it"
"What specifically — money, trust, or timing?"
"I have to talk to my partner"
"Smart. Let's get them on right now — 5 minutes."
"I already paid another company"
"Sunk cost. The only question now is dead leads vs. closed leads."
"Just send me info"
"I will, but you won't read it. One question — what do you need to see to say yes?"
"It's too expensive"
"Compared to what? Doing nothing costs you $X a month in missed jobs."
"I don't trust AI / agencies"
Don't argue — DEMO it. Let them hear it work. (Next slide.)
🎤 Talk track

These responses are reflex, not improv — drill them till they're automatic. Notice the last one; it's the most important move in the whole deck.

The Masterclass Move

Demo, don't debate

When a buyer holds a belief — "AI is garbage," "agencies are scams" — you don't out-argue it. You replace it with an experience.

From a recent close: He said "I refuse AI, it's garbage." We didn't argue — we called the live AI and let him hear it handle a price objection and upsell a ceramic coating. He flipped to "I need to set it up."
A 30-second demo beats a 30-minute argument.
🎤 Talk track

This is the move that wins the hard ones. You will never argue someone out of a belief — but you can replace it with proof. Always have a demo loaded and ready.

Phase 7 · Commitment

Never end with "I'll reach out"

Every call ends one of 3 ways
1
Closed today
2
Booked next step — date + time on the calendar now
3
Future-dated firm follow-up they agree to
Buying signals — pounce, don't keep pitching
  • "You're a smart guy / good deal"
  • Asking about price, timing, or start date
  • "How soon can you start?"
  • Repeating your terms back to you
  • Asking how billing or onboarding works

The call isn't over until a date exists.

🎤 Talk track

"I'll reach out" is a dead deal. Lock a specific date before you hang up. And the second you hear a buying signal — stop selling and start closing.

Closer vs. Order-Taker

Habits that close vs. habits that stall

Closers do

  • Ask for the close 3 times
  • Quantify the pain in dollars
  • Isolate the one real objection
  • Demo a belief instead of debating it
  • Take the slice, then upsell later
  • Lock a date before hanging up

Order-takers do

  • Feature-dump the whole offer
  • Accept "just send me info"
  • Defend the bundle and lose the slice
  • Argue about AI instead of showing it
  • Talk features, never outcomes
  • End on "let me know"
🎤 Talk track

Self-grade every recorded call against this list. The left column is a closer; the right column is an order-taker waiting for the buyer to do their job.

The One-Line Lesson

Diagnose. Sell the outcome.
Ask 3 times. End with a date.

Drill before every call: write the prospect's #1 pain on a sticky note. Your only job is to make them say it twice.

Now go close.
